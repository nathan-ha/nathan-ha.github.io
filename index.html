Nathan Ha

Hello! My name is Nathan Ha. I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of California, Riverside.

My main interests lie in the lower-level side of tech–things like embedded systems and FPGAs. That being said, I am open to just about any tech stack–from Rust to React–I want to try it all eventually.

I have experience in C, C++, Java, Python, C#, and JavaScript, just to name a few, as well as familiarity with the real-world software development cycle through Jira, and Jenkins.

My first internship was for software engineering at Cubic Corporation in San Diego. During my internship at Cubic, I developed and tested a public transportation ticketing system written mostly using C++ and JavaScript. Working closely with various teams, I resolved many issues to ensure seamless system deployment, and I strengthened my problem-solving skills in high-pressure, fast-paced environments.

During the school year, I am an Associate Engineer for the FSAE Highlander Racing Team. I contributed to the development of the Vehicle Control Unit and Battery Management System for an electric race car. I utilized my knowledge in both C and C++ programming to work on the firmware for ST and TI boards.

If you managed to read all that, thank you. Please check out my projects (click here) to see some stuff I've worked on.
